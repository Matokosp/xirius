About

Prisma

The following project is presented by PRISMA: Art, Science, Technology, which consists of an inter-institutional initiative based in Santiago de Chile dedicated to promote the investigation, production and exhibition of different kinds of works located at the intersections of art, science and technology with particular interest in nature. PRISMA has been collaboratively founded by the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile and University of Chile in 2019, with the aim of making visible theoretical and practical research related to transdisciplinary convergence between the aforementioned fields. PRISMA has been created by three specialists whose respective research intersects art, science and technology: Alejandro Jofré (PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of Pau – France, and Principal Researcher of the Center for Mathematical Modeling at University of Chile); Valentina Serrati (media artist, researcher and teacher, MA in Digital Media from Goldsmiths University – London, and Head of Extension of the Art Schools at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile); and Jazmín Adler (art historian, researcher and curator, PhD in Comparative Theory of Arts from the National University of Tres de Febrero, UNTREF - Buenos Aires; postdoctoral fellow at the Argentine Council for Scientific and Technical Research, and professor at the BA and MA in Electronic Arts – UNTREF).

XIRIUS is a virtual platform for navigation among agents who dialogue in the orbits of scientific knowledge and the arts. The project is based on Canis Major constellation with its star Sirius, which lights up its nose like the hound in the Hunter constellation. This dog is a seeker who helps his master to find what he needs; at the same time this star is the most luminous in the southern hemisphere when spring arrives, being a sign for the native peoples of the time of sowing and planting.

In this space XIRIUS is a link between art and science projects already in feedback with each other, as well as in potential collaboration. The structure of XIRIUS works on the basis of diverse origins projects that are transformed into nodes of connection open to interaction, forming constellations of knowledge in different stages activity, sharing experiences, data, dialogues and possibilities; assuming a notion of complementary and sustainable development in constant enrichment.
